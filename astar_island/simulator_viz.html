1
Food Production
phases/food.rs
formula — applied to every alive settlement
Δfood = food_plains × n_plains × (1 − food)
        + food_forest × n_forest × (1 − food)
        + food_mountain × n_mountain × (1 − food)
        + food_settlement × n_settlement × (1 − food)
        + food_ocean × n_ocean × (1 − food)
        + food_feedback × food × (1 − food)
        + food_pop_coeff × population
        + tech_food_coeff × tech_level × (1 − food)

food ← clamp(food + Δfood, 0, 0.998)
n_plains/forest/mountain/settlement/ocean = count of that terrain type among the 4-connected (cardinal) neighbours. Empty+Plains count as plains; Settlement+Port+Ruin count as settlement. The (1 − food) factor creates logistic saturation: terrain production slows as food approaches capacity. Population drains food linearly (no saturation). Tech level provides additional food production via the logistic term.
priors (from fixed-effects regression on replay data, R²≈0.97)
food_pop_coeff = −0.113  (linear pop drain)
food_feedback = +0.91  (coeff on food×(1−food))
food_plains = +0.043  (coeff on n_pl×(1−food))
food_forest = +0.074  (forest ≈ 1.7× plains; coeff on n_fo×(1−food))
food_mountain = 0.0  (negligible)
food_settlement = −0.017  (crowding penalty; coeff on n_st×(1−food))
food_ocean = 0.01  (coastal fishing; coeff on n_oc×(1−food))
tech_food_coeff = 0.05  (tech-gated food boost; coeff on tech×(1−food))
Fixed-effects regression on 126k replay observations proves the logistic form: terrain×(1−food) creates self-limiting growth that saturates as food→1. The food×(1−food) feedback term amplifies mid-range production (peak at food=0.5). Population drains food linearly — it does NOT scale with (1−food). Mountain and ocean adjacency have no measurable effect. Weather is i.i.d. per step, independent across seeds, approximately uniform (kurtosis≈1.9 < 3), std≈0.03–0.05.

Population, Defense, Ports & Longships
phases/pop_defense.rs
population — logistic growth + wealth boost
Δpop = pop_growth_rate × food × (1 − pop / pop_max) + pop_wealth_coeff × wealth × pop
population ← max(pop + Δpop, 0.01)
starvation — population decline when food is critically low
if food < collapse_threshold:
  population −= starvation_rate × pop   (floor 0.01)
Growth has two drivers: logistic food-gated growth (self-limiting at pop_max), plus a wealth-proportional term that scales with existing population. When food drops below the collapse threshold, the settlement bleeds population via the starvation rate.
defense — recovery proportional to population
Δdef = defense_recovery_rate × population
defense ← clamp(defense + Δdef, 0, 1.0)
settlement → port upgrade
n_ocean = count of 4-adjacent (cardinal) ocean tiles
if !has_port and n_ocean > 0:
  prob = port_upgrade_prob × pop × food × (1 + port_ocean_bonus × n_ocean)
  if roll < prob → has_port = true, grid cell → Port
Coastal settlements organically become ports when population and food are high. The probability scales with the number of adjacent ocean tiles via port_ocean_bonus, so triple-coast settlements are much more likely to upgrade. Wealth is not a factor.
port → longship building
if has_port and wealth ≥ longship_cost:
  prob = longship_build_prob × wealth
  if roll < prob → longships += 1, wealth -= longship_cost
Ports invest wealth into longships. Wealthier ports build faster (prob ∝ wealth). Each longship costs a fixed amount of wealth. Longships extend raid range in the conflict phase.
priors
pop_growth_rate = 0.06   pop_max = 5.0   pop_wealth_coeff = 0.01
starvation_rate = 0.05  (pop loss/step when food < collapse_threshold)
defense_recovery_rate = 0.02
port_upgrade_prob = 0.05   port_ocean_bonus = 0.3
longship_build_prob = 0.10   longship_cost = 0.10
Defense recovery is proportional to population, not (1−def). Larger settlements rebuild defenses faster. The rate varies ~5× across rounds (0.012–0.060). R² > 0.99 in all 9 rounds; coefficient stable to 6th decimal across seeds within a round. Starvation creates a death spiral when food drops too low: pop loss → less defense recovery → more vulnerable to raids.

Settlement Expansion
phases/growth.rs
spawn check — per alive settlement (shuffled order)
p_spawn = growth_prob × population × food × (1 + tech_growth_coeff × tech_level)
roll ~ Uniform(0,1) → spawn if roll < p_spawn
Both pop and food must be high to expand. Tech level provides an additional multiplier. A settlement with food=0.5, pop=1.0, tech=0 has p_spawn = 0.18 × 0.5 × 1.0 = 0.09.
target selection — weighted reservoir sampling
candidates = cells within taxicab(spawn_distance_max)
             where terrain ∈ {Plains, Forest}
             and no alive settlement occupies it

weight(cell) = exp(−spawn_distance_decay × taxi_dist)
             × spawn_weight_terrain
             × (1 + spawn_terrain_bonus × adj_food_tiles)

terrain weights: Plains=1.0, Forest=spawn_weight_forest
adj_food_tiles = count of adjacent Plains + Forest tiles
target ← one-pass weighted reservoir sample (zero-alloc)
child settlement stats
pop=child_pop, def=0.2
food = parent.food × 0.2, wealth = parent.wealth × 0.2
tech_level = 0, longships = 0
inherits parent's owner_id
parent cost
parent.population −= parent_cost
(parent must have pop > parent_cost to spawn)
post-growth port upgrade (all settlements)
for each alive non-port settlement with n_ocean > 0:
  prob = port_upgrade_prob × pop × food × (1 + port_ocean_bonus × n_ocean)
  if roll < prob → upgrade to Port
priors
growth_prob = 0.18   tech_growth_coeff = 0.05
spawn_distance_max = 3.0 cells (taxicab)
spawn_distance_decay = 0.3  (exp decay rate)
spawn_weight_forest = 0.8
spawn_terrain_bonus = 0.1  (per adj food tile)
child_pop = 0.5
parent_cost = 0.3
Spawn weighting uses exponential distance decay × terrain preference × adjacent food bonus, biasing toward nearby food-rich cells. Wealth at spawn = parent_wealth × 0.2 was proved by regression on 12k growth spawns (median ratio = 0.200, 25% exact ±0.001). Port upgrade runs again after growth, giving newly-spawned coastal settlements a chance to become ports immediately.

Raiding & Takeover
phases/conflict.rs
raid range — longship-dependent (taxicab distance)
range = raid_range + (longships > 0 ? longship_range_bonus : 0)
targets = alive settlements within taxicab range, different owner
raid probability — hunger-scaled, distance-decayed
hunger = max(1 − food, 0)
distance_factor = exp(−raid_distance_decay × taxi_dist)
p_raid = raid_prob × (1 + aggression × hunger) × distance_factor
roll ~ Uniform(0,1) → raid if roll < p_raid
Raid probability decays exponentially with taxicab distance to the target — nearby enemies are far more likely to be raided. Hunger amplifies aggression: starving settlements (food=0) have p_raid multiplied by (1 + aggression).
damage — computed from attacker & defender stats
damage = raid_damage × (1 + att.tech_level) × (1 + att.longships) × att.pop
          / ((1 + def.defense) × (1 + def.pop))
Damage scales with attacker tech, longships, and pop. High defender defense and pop reduce damage. The raid_damage param is the base coefficient.
raid effects — each with a learnable coefficient
defender.defense −= raid_defense_coeff × damage   (floor 0)
defender.pop ×= raid_pop_survival   (always, floor 0.01)
defender.total_damage += damage   (cap 1.0)
stolen = raid_steal_coeff × damage × def.wealth
attacker.wealth += stolen, defender.wealth −= stolen
defender.food ×= 1 − raid_food_loss × damage   (floor 0)
attacker.pop ×= 1 − raid_attacker_pop_loss × damage   (floor 0.01)
if damage > takeover_threshold
roll ~ Uniform(0,1)
roll < takeover_prob → Takeover (owner changes)
roll ≥ takeover_prob → Death → grid becomes Ruin
priors
raid_prob = 0.25
raid_range = 3.0  (taxicab)
longship_range_bonus = 2.0  (longships extend range to 5.0)
aggression = 1.5
raid_distance_decay = 0.2  (exp decay per taxi cell)
takeover_threshold = 0.25
takeover_prob = 0.40  (learnable, was hardcoded)
raid_damage = 0.25  (base coefficient)
raid_defense_coeff = 1.0
raid_pop_survival = 0.85
raid_steal_coeff = 1.0
raid_food_loss = 0.1  (food destroyed per damage)
raid_attacker_pop_loss = 0.05  (attacker casualties)
The takeover/death split is now learnable (prior 40/60). Raid probability decays with distance, so nearby enemies are raided far more often than distant ones. Raids now inflict food loss on defenders and population loss on attackers — raiding has a cost. Longships are the range extender (not ports), connecting build-in-phase-2 → use-in-phase-4.

Port Trade
phases/trade.rs
conditions for a trade pair
both settlements must have has_port = true
both must be alive
same owner_id
taxicab dist ≤ trade_range
bonus per qualifying pair
food += trade_food_bonus   (capped at 0.998)
wealth += trade_wealth_bonus
tech diffusion per qualifying pair (one-way up)
if port_b.tech > port_a.tech:
  port_a.tech += tech_diffusion_rate × (port_b.tech − port_a.tech)
elif port_a.tech > port_b.tech:
  port_b.tech += tech_diffusion_rate × (port_a.tech − port_b.tech)
clamped to [0, 1] after all pairs applied; no port loses tech
Tech only flows upward — the lower-tech port gains toward the higher. No port is penalized for trading with a less advanced partner. Multiple trade partners accelerate convergence.
priors
trade_range = 5.0
trade_food_bonus = 0.02 per pair per step
trade_wealth_bonus = 0.01 per pair per step
tech_diffusion_rate = 0.1
Trade only happens between same-faction ports — enemy ports cannot trade. Bonuses accumulate per pair, so a port with 3 friendly trading partners gains +0.06 food/step — comparable to the food_base. Tech diffuses only through port trade networks, creating a tech→raid damage feedback loop (phase 2 builds longships, phase 4 uses tech for damage, phase 5 spreads tech).

Winter
phases/winter.rs
food loss — applied to every alive settlement
// stochastic severity: i.i.d. per step, shared by all settlements
severity ~ Uniform(−winter_severity×√3, +winter_severity×√3)

food ← max(food − severity, 0)
collapse check
if food < collapse_threshold:
  grid[y][x] ← Ruin
  settlement.alive ← false
  disperse pop to neighbours
population dispersal
neighbours = alive same-faction settlements
            within taxicab dist ≤ winter_dispersal_range
each neighbour.pop += dead_pop / n_neighbours
priors
winter_severity = 0.035  (std of per-step uniform draw; i.i.d., independent across seeds)
collapse_threshold = 0.08
winter_dispersal_range = 3.0  (taxicab; learnable)
The dispersal radius is now a learnable parameter (prior 3.0, taxicab distance). Winter severity is stochastic: each step a new value is drawn from a uniform distribution (std ≈ 0.035), shared by all settlements but independent across seeds and i.i.d. over time. The distribution is approximately uniform (kurtosis ≈ 1.9, lighter tails than Gaussian). Positive draws reduce food; negative draws increase it — so winter is not always harsh.
Winter is the primary cause of settlement death. New ruins created here (and in conflict) are NOT eligible for environment transitions until the next step — the phase snapshot is taken at the start of each tick.

Ruin Transitions
phases/environment.rs
eligibility
only ruins that existed before this step
(ruins from conflict/winter this tick → next step)
transition probabilities per ruin
outcomeprobabilitycondition
→ Settlement ruin_to_settlement × n_nearby n_nearby = count of alive settlements within reclaim_range
→ Port ruin_to_settlement × n_nearby × 0.1 adjacent ocean required
→ Forest ruin_to_forest always
→ Plains residual 1 − others (most common)
new settlement stats (hardcoded)
population = 0.4
defense = 0.15
food = nearest_alive_settlement.food × 0.2
wealth = 0
owner_id = nearest alive settlement's owner
priors
ruin_to_settlement = 0.47
ruin_to_forest = 0.17
reclaim_range = 3.0 cells

p_settlement scales with nearby count (e.g. 3 nearby → 3 × 0.47)
Without nearby settlements, p_settlement = 0 — isolated ruins can only become Forest or Plains. With 1 nearby the base rate is 0.47; with 3 nearby it jumps to 1.41 (nearly certain reclamation). The port probability is 10% of settlement probability (hardcoded multiplier). New settlements from ruins use hardcoded stats (pop=0.4, def=0.15), distinct from growth-spawned children (pop=child_pop, def=0.2). Food is seeded from the nearest neighbour rather than a parent. Ruin reclaim food = nearest_food × 0.2 was proved on 10k ruin reclaims (median ratio = 0.200000, 47% exact ±0.001; phase 7 is last so no post-spawn modification). See data-analysis/prove_ruin_food.py.
